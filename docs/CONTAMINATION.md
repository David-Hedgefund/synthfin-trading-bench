# Contamination & Fairness Statement

Training-data contamination is the central threat to any LLM benchmark: if a model has seen the
test data (or its answers) during training, the score measures memorization, not capability. For
trading benchmarks this is acute — real market history is thoroughly represented on the web, so a
model can "predict" a 2020 crash it effectively memorized.

## Why this benchmark is contamination-free

1. **The data does not exist outside this benchmark.** Every price, fundamental, and news article is
   generated by the SynthFin engine and has not been published on the open web. There is no corpus
   for a model to have trained on.
2. **The future is invented, not historical.** Even scenarios themed on historical regimes (GFC,
   COVID) are *re-simulated* with synthetic tickers, paths, and text — the specific numbers a model
   is scored on were never observed by anyone.
3. **Refreshable.** If leakage is ever suspected (e.g. the corpus is scraped after release), a new
   corpus with new seeds can be generated and the leaderboard re-run. The task is fixed; the data is
   disposable. This is the property static real-world benchmarks cannot offer.
4. **Symbols are labels, not identities.** Real tickers are reused only as realistic surface strings.
   A model's real-world knowledge of "NVDA" confers no advantage because synthetic-NVDA's prices,
   fundamentals, and news are unrelated to the real company.

## Residual leakage vectors we control for

- **Prior-knowledge shortcuts.** Because fundamentals/news are synthetic and internally consistent
  but not real, memorized real-world facts actively mislead rather than help. We recommend an
  ablation (news-on vs news-off) to confirm models are *using* the provided information, not priors.
- **Prompt/format leakage.** All models share one fixed, versioned prompt; no model-specific tuning.
- **Lookahead leakage.** Enforced structurally (date-gated observation, next-open execution) and
  tested. See METHODOLOGY §2–3.
- **Survivorship / selection.** The universe is fixed at scenario start; no delisting-based
  survivorship bias.

## Fairness across providers

- Identical observation, identical prompt, identical action schema for every model.
- Decoding pinned (`temperature=0` default); model snapshot ids recorded verbatim.
- Providers with native JSON modes are used in JSON mode; parsing is tolerant and identical across
  providers, so no model is penalized for formatting.
- Baselines (buy-and-hold, equal-weight, momentum) are always run so absolute numbers are interpretable.

## What a good score does and does not prove

A high appraisal ratio here demonstrates that a model can **read point-in-time financial information
and translate it into risk-adjusted portfolio decisions in a controlled market** — with no
possibility of test-set memorization. It does **not** prove profitability in real markets, which
differ in microstructure, adversarial dynamics, and non-stationarity. Report both the capability
claim and this boundary.
