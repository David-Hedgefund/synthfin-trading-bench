# Data Card — SynthFin Trading Bench corpus

## Overview

The benchmark corpus is a set of **fully synthetic** market scenarios produced by the SynthFin
generation engine. Each scenario is a self-consistent multi-year market: daily price paths,
quarterly fundamentals, earnings, guidance, analyst estimates, corporate actions, and a stream of
dated unstructured documents (news, 10-K/10-Q MD&A, risk factors, 8-Ks, transcripts).

| | Validation corpus | **Published benchmark** |
|---|---|---|
| Tickers | 50 | **~1000** (operating) |
| Scenarios | 10 | **10** |
| Horizon per scenario | ~2 years (~560 trading days) | ~1.6 years (~400 trading days) |
| Doc types | news, mdna_10k/10q, risk_factors_10k, earnings/dividend/split 8-K, transcript | same |
| Seeds | 70000–70009 (curated) | 80000/11/13/14/17/18/19/21/22/23 (research) |

The 1000×10 corpus is 10 scenarios (research-panel seeds 80000/11/13/14/17/18/19/21/22/23) from
the larger 1000×25 run, with the full structured field set regenerated by the **generator-v0.1**
engine the documents were grounded on (verified: regenerated statements match the docs' quoted
figures exactly). Operating tickers only (no ETFs — ETF basket pricing is a later generator
version; the scorer uses its equal-weight-universe market proxy).

**Hosting:** private Hugging Face dataset `daviddata1/synthfin-trading-bench-1000x10`
(`synthfin_1000x10.zip`, 3.45 GB — 10 scenarios, ~130k structured files + ~520k LLM docs).
**Version (frozen):**
`sha256:4fda3b6ed7f5e03df5b07d45ea832be661fdd5f3dc81ce21b8cd6118f6ac9e21`. Cite this in every
leaderboard run. Download, unzip to a directory, and point `corpus_path` at it.

Ten research-panel macro regimes (seeds 80000–80023), chosen to span calm, bull, bear and crisis:

## Layout (standard SynthFin export)

```
<corpus>/
  index.json                      navigation: scenarios, ids, seeds, tickers, doc counts
  structured/scenarios/<slug>/
    scenario.json  macro.json
    tickers/<TICKER>/
      prices.json                 daily OHLCV + adjusted_close + per-bar return decomposition
      statements_quarterly.json  earnings.json  guidance.json  analyst_estimates.json
      metrics_ttm.json  company.json  corporate_actions.json
  unstructured/scenarios/<slug>/<TICKER|MARKET>/<doc_type>/<period>.json
```

### Price bar fields used by the benchmark
`time, open, high, low, close, adjusted_close, volume`, plus
`bar_metadata.return_components` — the generator's labeled return drivers (market, stress_beta,
sector, idiosyncratic, jump, aftershock, event, drift, scenario_tape, stress_packet, tail_cluster,
self_exciting_jump_cluster, mean_reversion). See METHODOLOGY §4.3 for how these are used.

### Document fields
`ticker, doc_type, date, period, title, text`. The `date` field is the availability date used for
point-in-time gating.

### Which sources the benchmark feeds the model
All of them, each gated by its own date — prices, 10-Q financials, earnings, **analyst estimates**,
guidance, **13F institutional holdings**, **insider (Form 4) trades**, and the news/filing/transcript
text. The exact mapping is in [`METHODOLOGY.md`](METHODOLOGY.md) §2. Two caveats:

- **`metrics_ttm.json` is *not* consumed** — it is a single end-of-scenario snapshot (no availability
  date) and would leak the future. TTM figures are recomputed from filed 10-Qs instead.
- **`institutional_holdings.json` (13F) and `insider_trades.json` (Form 4)** are emitted by the
  SynthFin bundler and consumed automatically when present, but the sample `synthfin_50x10` export
  was bundled without them. Ensure the 1000×10 bundle includes ownership panels if you want the model
  to see 13F/insider data (they are part of `archive_10x10_corpus.py`'s standard output).

## Provenance & integrity

- **Generator:** SynthFin engine, event-conditioned GJR-GARCH Student-t factor pricing model over a
  deterministic virtual universe. Fundamentals, prices, and news are cross-consistent
  (reconciled market cap, split-consistent prices, news grounded in issuer history).
- **Determinism:** each scenario is reproducible from its seed and generator version.
- **Content hash:** the published corpus ships with a SHA-256 content hash
  (`python scripts/hash_corpus.py <corpus>`); cite it as the dataset version. Any change to any
  price or document changes the hash.

## Intended use & limitations

- **Intended use:** benchmarking sequential decision-making of trading agents in a
  contamination-free setting; ablations on information access; regime-conditioned analysis.
- **Not intended:** as a source of real-market facts, backtesting real strategies for deployment, or
  training signals for live trading. Tickers reuse real symbols as labels for realism; **the data is
  invented and does not describe the real companies.**
- **No PII.** All documents are machine-generated; any resemblance to real filings is stylistic.
